The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Strategic Stability and Nuclear Risk: Volume III South Asian Perspectives

Reference: The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Strategic Stability and Nuclear Risk: Volume III South Asian Perspectives, Apr. 1, 2020, pp. 11-16 (8 pages)
https://www.jstor.org/stable/resrep24515

In this comprehensive volume, the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on strategic stability and nuclear risk is examined from South Asian perspectives. The authors delve into the potential ramifications of AI advancements in the context of nuclear-capable aerial and naval forces. The publication explores the intersection of AI and nuclear weapons delivery systems, offering insights into the evolving landscape of military capabilities and the associated risks. The analysis sheds light on the strategic implications of AI integration in the South Asian region.

Milrem Robotics: Revolutionizing Defense with Cutting-Edge Technology

Reference: https://milremrobotics.com/defence/

Milrem Robotics is at the forefront of the defense industry, pioneering the development of advanced robotic systems. With a focus on unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs) and military robots, Milrem Robotics offers a range of innovative solutions designed to enhance defense capabilities. Their expertise lies in creating versatile and modular platforms like THeMIS (Tracked Hybrid Modular Infantry System) and Type-X, which serve as integral components of modern military operations. By leveraging cutting-edge technology, Milrem Robotics is shaping the future of defense.

The Rise of Robots in the Chinese Military: Enhancing Capabilities and Innovation

Reference: https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202201/1246094.shtml

This article highlights the growing significance of robots in the Chinese military. With a focus on the "Robotic Yak," a large electric-powered quadruped robot designed for carrying supplies and munitions, China is pushing the boundaries of military innovation. The Global Times explores the capabilities and advancements of this robot, including its impressive load-carrying capacity and high speed. This development signifies China's commitment to incorporating cutting-edge technology in its defense strategy.

Robots at War: The New Battlefield

Reference: Robots at War: The New Battlefield, P. W. Singer, The Wilson Quarterly (1976-), Vol. 33, No. 1 (Winter, 2009), pp. 30-48 (19 pages)

In this thought-provoking article, P. W. Singer explores the emerging role of robots in modern warfare. Singer delves into the ethical, legal, and strategic implications of using robots on the battlefield. The article highlights various examples of military robots, their applications, and the potential challenges they pose. Singer raises critical questions regarding the use of robots in combat, emphasizing the need for careful consideration of the implications and consequences of this rapidly advancing technology.

Hacking Risks in Military Robots: Safeguarding Against Cyber Threats

Reference: https://www.21stcentech.com/major-problem-military-robots-hacked/

This article sheds light on the major cybersecurity challenge faced by military robots: the risk of being hacked. As military robots become increasingly integrated into warfare, their vulnerability to cyber attacks becomes a pressing concern. The article explores the potential consequences of hacked military robots, including the possibility of turning an entire army against its own country. The author emphasizes the importance of implementing robust cybersecurity measures to safeguard these critical defense assets.
